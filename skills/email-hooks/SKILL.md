# Email Hooks

- Email has two hooks before any content is read: the **subject line** (earns the open) and the **first line** (earns the read).
- The inbox is a feed — the subject competes with dozens of others in a half-second scan.
- Built on the six levers — see **storytelling-hooks**. Recap: 1. Curiosity gap · 2. Emotional mirror · 3. Conflict engine · 4. Relatability · 5. Pattern + surprise · 6. Three-act.

## When to Use

- Writing a subject line, preview text, or email opener.
- Newsletters, broadcasts, sequences, cold/warm outreach, launches.
- Diagnosing low open rate (subject problem) or low click rate (body/CTA problem).
- Any niche or business.

## The Inbox Stack (Quick Reference)

| Element | Job | Lever focus |
|---------|-----|-------------|
| Subject line | Earn the open | 1, 4, 5 |
| Preview text | Extend the gap (don't repeat subject) | 1, 2 |
| First line | Earn the read, open a loop | 1, 4 |
| Body | Build tension → deliver value | 3, 6 |
| CTA | One action | 1 |

## Subject Line Formulas

- **Curiosity gap:** "The mistake hiding in your [thing]."
- **Specificity:** "How I [result] with [oddly specific detail]." Concrete > vague.
- **Stakes:** "Don't [common action] until you read this."
- **Pattern break:** "Forget [common advice]."
- **Question that's 'about me':** "Is your [thing] secretly [problem]?"
- Rules:
  - Short — front-load the hook word; mobile truncates ~30–40 chars.
  - Lowercase/personal tone often outperforms shouty caps.
  - One idea; avoid two competing gaps.
  - No false promise — the body must pay it off or trust (and deliverability) erodes.

## Preview Text Pairing

- The preview text is a **second line of the hook**, shown next to the subject.
- Make it *extend* the subject's gap, never restate it.
- If left empty, clients pull the first body line — so write it deliberately.

## The Opening Line

- Skip greetings and warm-up; open a loop in the first sentence.
- Mirror a feeling the reader already has ("If your inbox stresses you out before 9am…").
- Short first sentence. White space invites the read.

## Body Flow

- Three-act: relatable setup → tension/problem → resolution that leads to the CTA.
- One idea per email; ruthless cuts. Skimmable: short paragraphs, line breaks.
- Build toward a single decision — the CTA is the resolution of the body's loop.

## The CTA

- **One** action per email. Competing CTAs split attention and kill clicks.
- Make it specific and low-friction ("Reply 'yes'", "Grab your spot", one link).
- Optionally bookend: echo the subject's phrase right before the CTA.

## Email Checklist

- [ ] Subject opens a gap and fits mobile truncation?
- [ ] Preview text extends (not repeats) the subject?
- [ ] First line opens a loop, no warm-up filler?
- [ ] Body is one idea, skimmable, rising toward the CTA?
- [ ] Exactly one clear, low-friction CTA?
- [ ] Does the email deliver on the subject's promise?
- Low opens → fix the subject/preview. Low clicks → fix the body/CTA.
