# Titles & Thumbnails

- The click is one decision made in under a second — title and thumbnail are designed **together as a pair**, never in isolation.
- They must **complement, not repeat**: the thumbnail shows the emotion/stakes, the title names the gap (or vice versa). If both say the same thing, you waste half the real estate.
- Built on the six levers — see **storytelling-hooks**. Recap: 1. Curiosity gap · 2. Emotional mirror · 3. Conflict engine · 4. Relatability · 5. Pattern + surprise · 6. Three-act.

## When to Use

- Writing a video title or a batch of title variations.
- Designing or briefing a thumbnail concept.
- Diagnosing low click-through rate (CTR) on existing uploads.
- Any niche: tutorials, vlogs, business, finance, faith, entertainment.

## The Pairing Principle (Quick Reference)

| Thumbnail does | Title does |
|----------------|------------|
| Emotion + stakes (visual) | The curiosity gap (words) |
| One focal subject | The specific promise/number |
| Shows the "what" | Asks/implies the "why/how" |

- **Test:** cover the title — does the thumbnail still raise a question? Cover the thumbnail — does the title still pull? Both should pass.

## Title Formulas

- **Curiosity gap:** "The one [thing] nobody tells you about [topic]."
- **Stakes/loss:** "This [mistake] is costing you [outcome]."
- **Result-first:** "How I [result] in [timeframe] (without [expected cost])."
- **Pattern break:** "Everyone does [common thing]. Here's why it's wrong."
- **Specific number:** "[N] [things] that [outcome]." Concrete numbers beat vague ("7 settings" > "some settings").
- **Question gap:** "Why does [surprising thing] happen?"
- Rules:
  - Front-load the hook word — mobile truncates titles.
  - One idea per title; don't stack two gaps.
  - Promise something the video actually delivers (broken promises tank retention and the algorithm).
  - Write 5–10 variants, pick the one with the widest relatability + sharpest gap.

## Thumbnail Concept Patterns

- **Single focal point:** one subject, instantly readable at small size. Clutter loses.
- **Emotion on a face:** genuine expression (shock, joy, worry) triggers the emotional mirror.
- **Contrast / before-after:** two states side by side create instant conflict.
- **The object of curiosity:** show the unnamed "thing" partially or circled.
- **Scale/number callout:** a big bold number or stakes figure.
- **Directional cues:** eyes, arrows, or gaze pointing at the focal element.
- Craft rules:
  - High contrast, few colors, large readable text (≤3–4 words).
  - Readable as a thumbnail-sized image AND on desktop — check both.
  - Faces large; expressions exaggerated but believable.
  - Text on thumbnail must NOT duplicate the title — add the missing half.

## Curiosity Without Clickbait

- Clickbait = a gap the video never closes → high CTR, dead retention, suppressed reach.
- Honest hook = a real gap the video genuinely pays off.
- If the most dramatic frame isn't in the video, don't put it on the thumbnail.

## Click-Through Checklist

- [ ] Do title and thumbnail say *different* halves of the same story?
- [ ] Does the thumbnail read clearly at small/mobile size?
- [ ] One focal subject, genuine emotion or clear contrast?
- [ ] Title front-loads the hook word and fits mobile truncation?
- [ ] At least one concrete number or specific where facts allow?
- [ ] Is the promise something the video actually delivers?
- [ ] Did you write 5+ title variants before choosing?
