# Carousel Narratives

- A carousel is a story told one swipe at a time. Each slide's only job is to make the thumb swipe to the next.
- Slide 1 is a hook (treat it like a thumbnail + title); the last slide is the bookend + call to action.
- Built on the six levers — see **storytelling-hooks**. Recap: 1. Curiosity gap · 2. Emotional mirror · 3. Conflict engine · 4. Relatability · 5. Pattern + surprise · 6. Three-act.

## When to Use

- Writing an Instagram or LinkedIn carousel (5–10 slides).
- Repurposing a video script, blog, or tip list into a swipeable post.
- Diagnosing a carousel with high reach but low saves/swipe-through.
- Any niche: education, business, design, finance, fitness, faith, personal brand.

## Slide Map (Quick Reference)

| Slide | Role | Lever focus |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | Hook — stop the scroll, open the loop | 1, 4, 2 |
| 2 | Stakes — why this matters to *you* | 3, 4 |
| 3–N−1 | Value beats — one idea per slide | 5, 6 |
| N | Payoff + CTA + bookend | 6, 1 |

## Slide 1: The Hook

- One bold idea, large readable text, minimal words.
- Open a curiosity gap wrapped in a relatable feeling — same job as a video hook.
- Add a swipe cue ("swipe →", an arrow, or a sentence that cuts off).
- Patterns: bold claim, surprising stat, "Stop doing X", "N mistakes that…", a question that's clearly "about me".

## The Swipe-Momentum Rule

- **One idea per slide.** Two ideas = a reason to stop.
- End slides on a **mini open loop**: "...but there's a catch →".
- Keep each slide skimmable in ~2 seconds; the deck readable in ~15.
- Maintain a visual rhythm (consistent layout) so cognitive load stays low and the thumb keeps moving.

## The Middle (Value Beats)

- Each beat = a small three-act: claim → why → quick proof/example.
- Use **pattern + surprise** on at least one slide to create a memorable jolt.
- Order beats by rising value or rising tension; don't front-load the best and flatten after.
- Numbers and specifics make beats sharp ("cut 3 steps" > "simplify").

## Final Slide: Payoff + CTA

- Resolve the loop opened on slide 1 (bookend — echo the opening phrase/number).
- **One** CTA only: save, share, comment a keyword, or follow. Pick the action that fits the goal:
  - Save → reference/how-to value.
  - Share → relatable/identity statement.
  - Comment → a question or keyword prompt.
- Make the action effortless and explicit.

## Caption Pairing

- The caption is a second hook for the feed preview — open a loop in the first line.
- Expand the story the slides compressed; end with the same CTA as the last slide.

## Carousel Checklist

- [ ] Does slide 1 stop the scroll and open a loop in ~2 seconds?
- [ ] Exactly one idea per slide?
- [ ] Does each slide end on momentum toward the next swipe?
- [ ] At least one pattern-break beat in the middle?
- [ ] Final slide pays off slide 1 (bookend) + a single clear CTA?
- [ ] First caption line is its own hook?
- If swipe-through is low: slide 1 is weak or a middle slide stalls — find the slide that closes a loop without opening one.
