# Long-Form YouTube Scripts

- Short-form wins the first 2 seconds; long-form must win the next 20 minutes.
- Retention is a chain of open loops: every section opens the next before it closes itself.
- Built on the six levers — see **storytelling-hooks** for the full psychology. One-line recap:
  1. Curiosity gap · 2. Emotional mirror · 3. Conflict engine · 4. Relatability · 5. Pattern + surprise · 6. Three-act structure.

## When to Use

- Writing a YouTube video script, tutorial, essay, story, review, or explainer.
- Planning structure/outline before scripting, or diagnosing a video that "loses people in the middle".
- Any niche: education, tech, finance, vlog, documentary, business, faith.

## Video Skeleton (Quick Reference)

| Section | Time | Job | Lever focus |
|---------|------|-----|-------------|
| Hook | 0:00–0:30 | Stop the click-away, make a promise | 1, 4, 2 |
| Setup | 0:30–1:30 | Frame the stakes, preview the payoff | 3, 6 |
| Body (chapters) | bulk | Deliver value as a loop chain | all |
| Sag point | ~40–60% | Re-hook before attention drops | 1, 5 |
| Payoff | near end | Resolve the opening promise | 6 |
| Outro/CTA | last 15s | One next action | 1 |

## The Opening (First 30 Seconds)

- **Cold open, no throat-clearing.** Cut the "hey guys, welcome back" — start in the hook.
- **State the promise/stakes** in plain words: what the viewer will gain or avoid.
- **Open a loop you only close at the end** (the bookend).
- **Optional preview flash:** tease the most dramatic moment from later ("by the end I lost everything — here's how I got it back").
- Patterns:
  - Result-first: show the outcome, then "here's how".
  - Question gap: pose the exact question the title implied.
  - Stakes: name what's at risk if they get this wrong.

## The Re-Hook / Open-Loop Chain

- **Rule:** never close a loop without opening a new one. Retention = unbroken chain.
- Plant **forward references**: "but that's not even the strange part — I'll get to it."
- Use **micro-cliffhangers** at chapter ends.
- **Callbacks:** pay off an earlier loop mid-video to reward watching, then open another.
- Track loops explicitly while scripting: list every loop, where it opens, where it closes.

## Chaptering & Pacing

- Break the video into **3–6 chapters**, each a mini three-act (setup → tension → resolution).
- One idea per chapter; name chapters as curiosity gaps, not labels ("The mistake that cost me $4k" > "Background").
- **Vary the rhythm:** alternate dense info with story/breath. Monotone pacing kills retention more than weak content.
- **Pattern interrupts** every 20–40s: B-roll, location/angle change, on-screen text, tone shift, a question to the viewer.
- Cut anything that doesn't advance a loop or pay one off.

## The Sag Point (40–60%)

- This is where most viewers drop. Treat it as a second hook.
- **Re-hook moves:** introduce the biggest twist, raise the stakes, drop a surprising fact, or restate the core promise with new urgency.
- Use **pattern + surprise** here: "Everything was working — until it wasn't."

## Payoff & Resolution

- Close the **opening loop explicitly** — viewers came for this.
- Deliver the lesson/feeling, not just facts (emotional mirror).
- **Bookend:** echo a phrase/number/image from the hook.
- Don't over-resolve — leave one resonant thought.

## Outro & CTA

- One action only (watch next / subscribe / link). Multiple CTAs = none followed.
- **Tease the next video** as a fresh curiosity gap to feed the session.
- Keep it under ~15 seconds; end-screen window.

## Retention Checklist

- [ ] Cold open — no intro filler before the hook?
- [ ] Clear promise + an opening loop closed only at the end?
- [ ] Every chapter opens the next before closing itself?
- [ ] A deliberate re-hook at the 40–60% sag point?
- [ ] Pattern interrupt roughly every 20–40 seconds?
- [ ] Pacing varies (dense ↔ story/breath)?
- [ ] Payoff resolves the opening promise + bookends it?
- [ ] Single CTA + next-video tease?
- If a chapter feels flat: which lever is missing? Usually conflict or a new loop.
